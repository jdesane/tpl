# The LPT Sponsor Checklist

**12 questions every agent should ask before picking a sponsor at LPT Realty.**

When you join LPT Realty, the agent who brings you in is your sponsor. They collect revenue share from your production for as long as you're in the brokerage. That makes sponsor selection one of the most financially significant decisions of your entire career.

The right sponsor turns that line item into real leverage - systems, coaching, lead flow, and a team building alongside you. The wrong sponsor is just a name on paperwork.

Use this checklist on every sponsor conversation. If the answers feel thin, keep looking.

---

## Section 1: Production and Credibility

You're picking someone to mentor your business. Start by confirming they have one.

**1. Are you an active, producing agent this year?**

A sponsor who isn't closing deals in the current market can't coach you through the current market. Ask for a rough transaction count in the last twelve months. "I help other agents grow their business" is not an answer to this question.

**2. How long have you been at LPT Realty, and what brokerage were you at before?**

You want someone who has lived the switch themselves. A sponsor who has been at LPT for two or three years has seen the plans evolve, has watched revenue share compound, and can tell you what to expect at month six versus month thirty-six.

**3. Can you share one or two recent wins from agents you've personally sponsored?**

Real examples with real names and real numbers. If nobody on their team has closed their first LPT deal, that is the data point. It does not mean you should rule them out, but it should shape what you expect.

---

## Section 2: Systems and Infrastructure

This is where sponsors separate themselves. A sponsor who hands you the LPT onboarding packet and disappears is not a sponsor - they're a rev share collector.

**4. What systems will I get access to on day one?**

Specifics matter here. CRM access, lead intake forms, listing checklists, buyer agreements, SOPs for common transactions. Ask to see a screen share if you can. A sponsor running a real operation can show you a real operation.

**5. Do you have a content or marketing engine I can plug into?**

Paid ads, organic content, a newsletter, a podcast, a YouTube channel, a social calendar. You do not need to use every asset. You do need to know what leverage is on the table. Content generates both leads and credibility, and you are unlikely to build all of it alone in your first year.

**6. Is there a shared lead pool, or am I on my own for pipeline?**

Some sponsors generate leads and distribute them to agents on their team. Some do not. Both can be legitimate models. You just need to know which one you are walking into so you can plan your first ninety days accordingly.

---

## Section 3: Coaching and Accountability

Systems are worthless without a rhythm of use.

**7. How often will we talk in my first ninety days?**

"Reach out any time" is not a coaching cadence. You want a weekly call, a group call, a group chat, office hours - something structured. The first ninety days at a new brokerage are the window where habits set. Sponsors who know this will have a clear answer.

**8. Do you run group coaching or an agent community?**

Peer learning is one of the most underrated parts of a brokerage change. A sponsor who has built a community of agents at your level (or just ahead of it) gives you a place to bring questions without feeling like you are burning one-on-one time.

**9. What's your policy on agents who are underperforming their plan?**

Listen for honesty here. A sponsor who says "I ride it out and hope" is telling you exactly how much accountability you can expect. A sponsor who runs real check-ins, resets plans, and is willing to have hard conversations will help you avoid the slow fade most switching agents quietly experience in year two.

---

## Section 4: Long-Term Alignment

The point of revenue share is long horizons. Make sure the math works for both of you.

**10. Are you building a team, or are you primarily focused on recruiting for revenue share?**

Both can work. Know the difference. A team builder invests in your production because your production is also their reputation. A pure recruiter's incentive is to sign you and move on to the next name.

**11. How do you handle it if I outgrow the sponsor relationship?**

The healthy answer is that the sponsor is supportive of agents evolving into their own team leaders, their own brand, their own rev share recruiting. Watch for sponsors who treat downstream agents as their property instead of their partners.

**12. What's your five-year vision for the agents on your team?**

You want to hear a vision that includes more than transaction volume. Wealth building, personal brand, equity, time freedom, family impact - the better sponsors have thought about where their agents are going, not just where they are today.

---

## One Final Test

After you've asked all twelve, ask yourself this: **Could I see myself on this person's team in three years and still feel good about it?**

If the answer is yes, you have found your sponsor. If the answer is "I think so," keep going. Revenue share compounds for decades. You have time to pick correctly.
