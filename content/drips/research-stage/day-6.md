Hey {{first_name}},

I spent a decade at Keller Williams before I moved to LPT. The move wasn't a protest. KW gave me the foundation I still use every day. But three things pushed me out, and it's worth sharing because you'll probably feel at least one of them yourself.

One: the fee math stopped making sense. Between the cap, royalty, and franchise piece, I was giving up roughly eighteen thousand dollars a year for a brand that no longer added eighteen thousand dollars of leverage to my business. That's not a KW problem. It's a stage-of-career problem.

Two: the support systems I needed weren't the systems the market center was designed to deliver. Great for new agents. Too generic for the phase I was in.

Three: no equity. I was generating revenue for a company I had no ownership in, and I wasn't going to fix that by staying.

If you're reading this and any of those three sound familiar, you already know what the next move is. I wish someone had framed it that cleanly for me instead of making me piece it together over six months of back-and-forth.

If you want to talk it through, just reply.
