Hey {{first_name}},

The part of the sponsor decision nobody talks about: it's not just a revenue share line item. It's an opportunity cost.

Picture two versions of your next twelve months.

Version one, you picked a sponsor who signs paperwork and disappears. You spend your first ninety days Googling "buyer rep Florida template" and rebuilding a CRM. You close deals. You're also doing the work of running a brokerage inside your business, alone. At the end of the year you look back and most of your energy went to infrastructure, not production.

Version two, you picked a sponsor with a real operation. You inherit a CRM, SOPs, a content engine, and a coaching cadence on day one. You still close deals. Only now you closed more of them, with less friction, and you spent your energy on the lever that actually moves income: conversations with clients.

Same brokerage. Same plans. Same fees. Wildly different years.

The revenue share you give to a good sponsor is paying for leverage. The revenue share you give to a bad sponsor is paying for paperwork. Both cost the same.

That's the decision.
