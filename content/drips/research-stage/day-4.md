Hey {{first_name}},

One of the most asked questions I get: "Explain revenue share like I'm going to have to run the math myself, because I am."

Short version: LPT pays revenue share seven levels deep on agents you personally sponsor, and the agents they sponsor, and so on. You get a percentage of the brokerage's revenue from each of those agents' production, not a cut of their commission. It's paid for as long as they're in the brokerage and you are too.

That structure has a specific implication most people miss. A single good sponsor decision by you, made once, compounds for years. You don't have to keep recruiting to keep getting paid. You have to keep one person happy enough to stay.

The flip side is the common trap: a lot of agents sign at a brokerage for the rev share, sponsor two or three people in the first year, and then wonder why it isn't moving. The math works over a seven-level deep tree, not on a shallow one.

I broke the full math and a real worked example down here: tplcollective.ai/revshare

Worth five minutes if you want to see the numbers play out.
