**Subject:** The first mistake I see new agents make

**Preview:** It's not the split. It's not the fees. It's this.

Hey {{first_name}},

The first mistake new agents make when picking a sponsor is optimizing for the commission split.

I get why. It's the number on the page. 70/30, 80/20, 100%, it feels like the thing you should negotiate.

Here's the problem: if you close zero deals your first year, a 100% split on zero is still zero. The split only matters when you have volume. And volume in year one comes from two things almost nobody asks sponsors about:

1. How many leads does the brokerage actually hand you, and under what terms
2. How many hours of real mentorship do you get from someone who's closed deals in your exact market

The agent who closes 8 deals in year one on a 70/30 split with real coaching will earn more than the agent who closes 1 deal on a 100% split with no support.

Ask sponsors the lead question and the mentorship question on the first call. Count how many concrete numbers they give you. "We have amazing training" is not a number. "You'll sit in on three listing presentations in your first 30 days and have a 30-minute 1-on-1 every Monday" is a number.

If the answers get vague, that's the answer.

More tomorrow on what a good sponsor actually does for you.
