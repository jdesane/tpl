**Subject:** The monthly-fee trap

**Preview:** Why $99/mo matters way more than 80/20 when you're new.

Hey {{first_name}},

Here's math nobody runs with you on a sponsor call.

Most traditional brokerages charge a monthly fee plus transaction fees plus E&O plus desk fees plus tech fees. At KW it averages around $150/month plus $35 per transaction plus $550 E&O. At RE/MAX it's often $400-$1,200/month plus transaction fees. At Coldwell Banker it's in the same range.

If you close zero deals in year one, you still owe all of that. Agents in their first year at those shops often pay $1,800 to $14,000 just to keep their license active before they earn a dollar.

The first-year math at LPT is different. $99/month flat, no E&O, no desk, no transaction fees, no franchise fees. Worst case year one, you're out $1,188.

That's not a pitch, it's arithmetic. The difference matters most when you're still building. Once you're doing volume, the split matters more than the monthly. But year one, the monthly is what keeps you in the game long enough to get to year two.

When you interview sponsors, ask them to add up every fee you'd pay if you closed zero deals in the next 12 months. Most will stall. The honest ones will give you a real number. Use the real number to compare.
