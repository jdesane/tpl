**Subject:** What a sponsor should actually do for you

**Preview:** Seven things. If your sponsor isn't doing most of them, find a new one.

Hey {{first_name}},

A sponsor is not a logo on your business card. It's the person who's legally responsible for your license and, if they're any good, the person shortcuts your first 12 months.

Here's what a real sponsor does. If you hear vague answers on most of these during your interview, keep looking.

1. Shadows your first 2-3 listing presentations and buyer consults in person or on Zoom
2. Reviews your first 5 contracts line by line before you send them
3. Puts you in a pipeline with real leads, not just "we have great marketing"
4. Gives you a clear path from agent to team lead to mentor, if that's what you want
5. Responds to a contract question within 2 hours during business hours
6. Tells you when something you're about to do is a bad idea, even if it costs them a deal
7. Knows your market. Not the state. The specific zip codes you'll be working.

The sponsors who can do all seven exist. They are also the ones who turn new agents away if the fit isn't right, which is actually a good sign. A sponsor who signs everyone usually has a volume business, not a development business.

A lot of new agents at LPT pair with someone on my team for the first year for exactly these reasons. No pressure, just an option if what you've seen from other sponsors hasn't felt right.
