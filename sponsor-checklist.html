Your sponsor is the agent who recruits you to LPT Realty. This relationship matters more than most new agents realize. A good sponsor costs you nothing and can accelerate your first year significantly. A bad one is just a name on your paperwork.

Use this checklist when you interview any potential sponsor - including Joe. If they can't answer these questions, that tells you everything you need to know.

Support & Training
What does your onboarding process look like in my first 90 days?
Good answer
A structured onboarding with specific milestones - tools setup, first transaction walkthrough, weekly check-ins.
Red flag
"You'll figure it out - LPT has a lot of resources online." Vague = you're on your own.
What TPL provides
Structured onboarding checklist with 10 milestones, tools setup walkthrough, Lofty CRM access, and direct access to Joe through the community.
Can I reach you directly when I have a question, or will I be directed to someone else?
Good answer
Direct access - they share their actual phone number or a clear way to reach them personally.
Red flag
"I have a team member who handles onboarding questions." You're being handed off before you even start.
What TPL provides
Direct access to Joe through the TPL community Discord. Not a customer service rep - the actual person who recruited you.
Are you actively selling real estate right now, or are you primarily focused on building your downline?
Good answer
Either honest answer is fine - as long as they're transparent. "I do both" or "I focus on coaching" is OK if they own it.
Red flag
Claiming to be an active producer with no deals to back it up. A sponsor who can't help you close transactions is a cheerleader, not a resource.
What TPL provides
Joe is transparent about his focus: TPL is a recruiting and coaching operation built on top of LPT Realty. The value is in the community infrastructure, not pretending to be a sales team.
Fees & Compensation
Walk me through every fee I'll pay - monthly, per transaction, and annual. What's my total cost in year one if I close 10 deals?
Good answer
A specific number with line-item breakdown. "$65/month + $295 per closing, E&O included. 10 deals = $3,730 total." No hedging.
Red flag
"It depends" or "LPT has a few options, check the website." If they can't quote you their own brokerage's fees, they don't know their own deal.
What TPL provides
The math is public at tplcollective.ai/fee-plans. Brokerage Partner: $65/mo + $295/closing. Professional: $0/mo + $395/closing. No royalty, no franchise fees.
How does LPT's revenue share work, and how much have you personally earned from it?
Good answer
Specific explanation of the tier structure (7 tiers, 5% down to 0.5%), and honest about their actual earnings - even if small.
Red flag
Vague claims about "massive passive income" with no specifics. Or saying revenue share "doesn't really matter for new agents" - that undersells a real long-term asset.
What TPL provides
Full revenue share explainer at tplcollective.ai/revshare. 7-tier structure, you earn from agents your recruits recruit. It compounds. Joe is building this actively and shares real numbers.
Does your sponsor earn money when I close a deal? How?
Good answer
Transparency. "Yes, LPT pays me a portion of the company dollar from your closings." Honest about alignment of interests.
Red flag
Claiming to be purely altruistic with no financial relationship. That's either false or means they're at a brokerage with no revenue share - which means even less incentive to help you.
What TPL provides
Full transparency: Joe earns revenue share from your closings through LPT's HybridShare program. The incentive is aligned - the more you produce, the better for everyone.
Tools & Technology
What tools and technology do I get access to at LPT, and what do I have to pay for separately?
Good answer
Clear list: what's included with LPT (like Lofty CRM, Dotloop, Dezzy.ai) vs what you'd upgrade separately.
Red flag
"LPT has great technology" with no specifics. Every brokerage claims this. Ask for the actual tool list.
What TPL provides
LPT includes: Lofty CRM, Dotloop transaction management, Dezzy.ai (AI tools). TPL adds: recruiting tools, content system, Mission Control dashboard for tracking your business.
Do you have any tools, templates, or systems beyond what LPT provides that I'd have access to?
Good answer
Specific tools, templates, scripts, or systems they've built or curated. "Here's our agent resource vault" beats a vague promise.
Red flag
"I'll connect you to some people." A network isn't a system. If there's nothing tangible, there's no infrastructure behind the sponsorship.
What TPL provides
TPL Resource Vault (scripts, templates, calculators), the tplcollective.ai/resources page, commission calculator tools, and a content library accessible through the agent portal.
Community & Culture
How many agents are currently in your downline and how often do you actively engage with them?
Good answer
A real number with context on how they stay connected - community group, regular calls, office hours.
Red flag
A very large number with no system for engagement. 500 agents with no community = you're agent number 501 they'll never talk to.
What TPL provides
TPL Collective community on Discord with regular engagement. Not thousands of ghosts - an active group Joe actually participates in. Size matters less than activity.
Can you connect me with one or two agents you've sponsored who are willing to talk about their experience?
Good answer
They offer referrals without hesitation. Confident sponsors have agents who will vouch for them.
Red flag
Deflection, excuses, or offering to show you testimonials instead of actual conversations. Testimonials are curated. Conversations are real.
What TPL provides
Ask Joe directly. He'll connect you with agents in TPL who can give you an honest read on what working with him looks like. No scripts.
Long-Term Growth
If I want to eventually build a team or recruit other agents, what does that path look like with you?
Good answer
A real roadmap: what they'll help you build, what the revenue share math looks like at scale, what support they provide for recruiting.
Red flag
"Just focus on selling for now." That's not a growth vision - that's a sponsor who doesn't plan to invest in your business beyond year one.
What TPL provides
This is the core of what TPL is built for. Joe has recruiting systems, content templates, and the full TPL infrastructure for agents who want to build a team or recruiting line. It's not an afterthought - it's the primary model.
What is your long-term vision for your group, and where do you see it in 3 years?
Good answer
A real answer - not "bigger and better." Specific goals, what they're building toward, how agents benefit as it grows.
Red flag
No vision at all. A sponsor with no plan is building nothing - you're signing up for a quiet transaction, not a real professional relationship.
What TPL provides
TPL is building a media and AI infrastructure layer on top of LPT Realty in Florida. The goal is a network of 100+ producing agents supported by content, tools, and automation. Each agent who joins strengthens the whole group.
Practical Questions
What happens if I have a transaction issue or need help on a deal and you're not available?
Good answer
A backup plan: broker support team at LPT, community of experienced agents you can tap, or named people who can help in a pinch.
Red flag
No answer. Every sponsor should know that LPT has a broker support team - and any good sponsor acknowledges that they're one person with limits.
What TPL provides
LPT has dedicated broker support for transaction questions. TPL agents also have the community as a resource. For complex issues, LPT's in-house compliance and legal support is available.
Why did you choose LPT Realty specifically, and what would make you leave?
Good answer
An honest, specific answer - both why they joined and what would actually move the needle to leave. Shows real conviction, not just enthusiasm.
Red flag
Pure cheerleading with no critical thought. "LPT is the best brokerage in the world!" without any personal evaluation suggests they're selling, not advising.
What TPL provides
Joe left KW after calculating $17,883 in annual savings at LPT. He has a clear answer. What would make him leave: if the fee structure changed materially or the platform stopped improving. He'll tell you exactly this if you ask.
What does the relationship look like after I'm licensed and active - weekly, monthly, ongoing?
Good answer
A real cadence - community access, regular touchpoints, what ongoing support looks like as you grow.
Red flag
"The door is always open." That phrase means nothing. What specifically will they do for you 6 months after you join?
What TPL provides
Ongoing: TPL community Discord (daily), agent portal with resources, content and marketing tools, monthly group touchpoints. The relationship doesn't end at signing - that's when it starts.
